Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The graphics on this page compare averages of each state in the United States on a variety of factors including the Obesity Rate, Percentage of Smokers, and Pertange of those who lack Healthcare to the Pertange of the Population in Poverty, the Median Age, and the Median Household Income in each state. One will notice a number of patterns in those states which have low income levels and or high poverty percentages in comparison to the percentage of obesity, percentage of smokers, and lack of healthcare.

A majority of states that have high poverty levels will tend to have higher levels of Obesity and Poverty. While states with lower poverty levels will have a lower percentage of citizens who lack healthcare. Similar to the poverty levels, those state that have higher income averages will tend to have lower obesity and smoking percentages, while the lower income states will have higher obesity and smoking percentages. At the same time one will notice that income levels does not play a role on whether or not the percentage of citizens who lack healthcare will be high or low. Except for one outlier, Texas.

In regards to age one will notice that the median age for each state in the United States is between the ages of 34-42. One last interesting results viewers will notice is that New York is located in the center/average for each factor selected.
